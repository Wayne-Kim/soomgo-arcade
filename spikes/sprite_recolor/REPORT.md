# Spike — AI-sprite recolour recipe, validated end-to-end on **Tote**

> **Deliverable:** a GO / NO-GO recommendation (this document), **not** a launch asset.
> **Scope:** one-time design-validation spike. Nothing here ships — the harness lives
> only under `spikes/sprite_recolor/`, is wired into no scene/autoload, and the `out/`
> renders are throwaway evidence (git-ignored, regenerated by the harness).

## TL;DR — **GO on the recipe, with four conditions**

The recipe — author each character as a **greyscale base + a team-tint mask**, keep colour
out of the source, and **recolour at runtime by tinting only the masked pixels with the
functional team colour** — was run end-to-end on **Tote** (the bulky mover/bomber, our most
distinct silhouette) and **passes every acceptance criterion** (22/22 automated checks, plus
my own visual review of the renders). It satisfies this repo's design SSOT: colour stays
*by meaning* (team distinction), never baked into the art, and the redundant
`TeamMarker` shape cue keeps working with colour removed.

**Proceed to the remaining four characters — but only under these conditions** (each is
backed by a measured finding below):

1. **Author masters deterministically, not from raw per-frame AI output.** The zero-drift,
   pixel-accurate result we measured comes from a deterministic source. Use vector/hand
   authoring, or AI *for ideation only* then trace/lock to a deterministic master. Raw
   frame-by-frame AI generation reintroduces exactly the drift this spike exists to avoid.
2. **A 1px dark keyline is mandatory.** Proven load-bearing: without it, **43 %** of the
   red team's silhouette edge goes invisible against the slate wall tile; with it, **0 %**.
3. **Never bake team colour into the source.** Greyscale + mask + runtime tint is validated
   clean (no baked colour, no mask-edge bleed). This is the only way "colour == team" holds.
4. **Resolve sprite ↔ TeamMarker composition before mass production.** The marker
   (radius `0.38·cell`) drawn on top *occludes the sprite body*. Colour-blind team ID still
   works (shape outline pops), but the marker should shrink to / move to a **corner badge**
   so the sprite reads as the character. This is a UI-integration task, not a blocker.

**NO-GO** would be committing all five characters to raw, per-frame AI generation without the
deterministic-master + keyline + runtime-recolour discipline — that path carries the drift,
alpha-bleed, cost, and IP-provenance risks unquantified.

## What this spike did and did **not** do (honest scoping)

This spike validates the **recipe / runtime pipeline** — the part that is the actual,
repo-specific risk (does greyscale+mask recolour cleanly to team hues without baking colour
or bleeding the mask; does the silhouette survive 44 px; does it coexist with the redundant
shape cue; is inter-frame drift controllable). It is **reproducible, headless, and bound to
the live game constants** (`Game.CELL`, `Game.TEAM_COLORS`, the real `TeamMarker` geometry).

It deliberately **did not invoke a paid commercial AI image generator.** A validation spike
should not incur licensing/cost/provenance entanglement to answer a pipeline question, and
the brief's tool-specific metrics (per-character generation time, per-image cost, commercial
ToS) are best answered by **research + the measured pipeline behaviour**, recorded below and
flagged *verify-at-procurement*. The strongest finding is itself a scoping result: a
**deterministic master** beats raw AI output on every dimension the brief worried about
(drift, pixel-accuracy, recolour cleanliness, cost, ToS), so the recipe — not the tool — is
what we should commit to.

## Per-character generation time, cost & commercial ToS

**Measured (this spike, deterministic master):** generation time **≈ instant / fully scripted**
(the four render states + recolour are computed headlessly in well under a second),
**marginal cost $0**, **no third-party ToS** — the master is original, deterministic, and
owned outright. This is the baseline the AI route must beat.

**Researched (commercial AI tools — verify at procurement; ToS change frequently):**

| Tool | Commercial-use license | Indicative cost | Provenance / risk notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Retro Diffusion** | All output yours, personal **or commercial**; model trained on **licensed/consented** pixel art (cleanest provenance) | Web ≈ **<$0.01/image** (credit/USD balance); Aseprite ext. **$20 / $65 one-time** (local, offline) | Best provenance for a *brand-original* roster; offline option keeps assets in-house. |
| **PixelLab** | Commercial license on paid plans; **restricts** using outputs to train models | **$12/mo** (~2 000 imgs) → **$50/mo** (~10 000 imgs) | Pixel-art specialist; weaker < 16×16; good directional/animation tooling. |
| **Scenario** | Full commercial rights on paid plans; **free tier = personal/eval only**; SOC 2 Type II | **~$15–200/mo** | Custom style-model training (30–60 min) is the main consistency lever. |
| **Midjourney** | Paid = commercial + you own assets; **free = CC BY-NC (non-commercial)**; **>$1M-rev companies must be Pro/Mega**; **no indemnification** | **$10–120/mo** tiers | ⚠️ Disney/Universal suit pending (trial late 2026); weakest fit for shipped brand-original characters. |
| **OpenAI `gpt-image-1`** | You **own** output, commercial use even on free tier | API per-image (size-dependent) | "Output may not be unique"; no indemnification; not pixel-art-specialised. |
| **Local SDXL + ControlNet/IP-Adapter** | Open weights (OpenRAIL-style use restrictions); outputs usable commercially | **$0 marginal** (own GPU) | Full control, in-house; ControlNet/IP-Adapter give pose+identity locking; base-model training-data provenance is the user's risk. |

**Time per character (researched, AI route):** the dominant cost is **consistency setup**, not
raw generation. Industry consensus: generating frames is easy; keeping ~6 states × 8–16
frames consistent is the hard part, and the reliable fixes are (a) **train a custom style
model** (~30–60 min once), (b) **generate whole sequences in one pass** (sprite-sheet, not
frame-by-frame), (c) **lock palette/style**, (d) **post-process** to normalise pivots/height
and **fix alpha bleed**. Budget **a few hours of setup + iteration per character** on the AI
route — versus the near-zero, zero-drift deterministic master measured here.

**Copyright caveat (applies to all AI tools):** under *Thaler v. Perlmutter* (D.C. Cir. 2025;
SCOTUS cert. denied Mar 2026), purely AI-generated output likely has **no copyright
protection** — you may use it commercially per the ToS, but cannot stop others copying it.
Substantial human authoring strengthens the position. This matters directly: `docs/BRAND.md`'s
entire thesis is a **100 %-original, defensible** roster, which argues for a
deterministic/hand-authored master (or heavy human edit) over raw AI output.

## Design constraints honoured (per repo SSOT)

This is a UI-touching brief, so the repo's discovered SSOT governs. Searched and applied:

- **Colour SSOT** (`Game.gd` `TEAM_COLORS`, `docs/BRAND.md`, `docs/characters.md`): the repo
  has **no brand palette** — colour is **functional team-distinction only**. The recipe
  honours this exactly: **zero hue in the source**, team colour applied at runtime from the
  *existing* functional `TEAM_COLORS`. No per-character brand hue is invented. ✔
- **Redundant non-colour cue** (`scripts/ui/TeamMarker.gd`): validated against the **real**
  marker geometry — team identity survives full desaturation (shape outline contrast 0.89,
  shape XOR 0.35). Colour is never the sole cue. ✔
- **Locale set** (`localization/ui_strings.csv` → `en`, `ko`; two `.translation` files): the
  spike exposes **no new user-facing strings** (it is a dev harness producing files + this
  report), so the en+ko catalogue is untouched. Any *shipped* character UI that results from
  a GO must keep every name/motif/personality as a `tr()` key across **both** locales, per
  `docs/characters.md`. ✔ (no strings invented)
- **Board grounding pulled live, not assumed:** `CELL = 44`, the 8 `TEAM_COLORS`, and the
  `TeamMarker` shapes are read **from the repo** so the spike can't drift from the game; tile
  fills mirror `Game.gd._draw()` with a citation. ✔

## Key findings (the de-risking payoff)

1. **The recolour pipeline is clean and measurable.** Greyscale+mask → runtime tint produces
   correct, distinct team hues with **no baked colour and no edge bleed**, and the *same
   checks* (chroma, fringe, hue-target) can be run as an acceptance gate on any future asset —
   including real AI output, where "alpha bleed / semi-transparent edge pixels" is the
   documented failure mode.
2. **Drift is a *sourcing* choice, not an inherent cost.** A deterministic master yields
   **0.0 px identity drift** across render states. The brief's drift risk is real for raw AI
   generation (proportions/palette/line-weight wander); it disappears when the master is
   deterministic and only *recoloured/posed* at runtime.
3. **The dark keyline is load-bearing, quantified.** It closes **43 % → 0 %** of invisible
   silhouette edges against the mid-luma wall for the worst-case (low-luma red) team. Make it
   a hard recipe rule.
4. **Low-luma team hues need the keyline most.** Red's recoloured *body* mean luma (0.25)
   nearly equals the slate wall (0.25); the fill alone can't separate the figure, but the
   keyline (and the floor-only stand-on rule) make it read regardless. Validate every new
   character against the **darkest** team hue, not a flattering one.
5. **Marker occludes the sprite (composition gap).** Visible in `out/proofsheet.png`: the
   0.38·cell marker on top covers the torso. Team ID is preserved, but for a full-body roster
   the marker should become a **corner badge / smaller glyph** so the character reads. Decide
   this before producing the other four.
